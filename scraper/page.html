Children's Stories & Fairy Tales

Frequently Asked Questions about Children's Stories

What are the best short stories for children?

Our most popular stories include The Three Little Pigs, Little Red Riding Hood, Goldilocks and the Three Bears, Jack and the Beanstalk, and The Tale of Peter Rabbit. Classic fairy tales by Hans Christian Andersen — like The Little Match Girl, The Ugly Duckling, and The Emperor's New Clothes — are also perennial favorites. For fables with moral lessons, try Aesop's The Tortoise and the Hare or The Ant and the Grasshopper.

Are these children's stories free to read online?

Yes! Every story in our collection is completely free to read online. No account, subscription, or download is required — just pick a story and start reading. We've been sharing classic literature with readers since 2000.

What age group are these stories appropriate for?

Our collection spans ages 2 through 12. Nursery rhymes and simple tales like The Three Billy-Goats Gruff are great for toddlers and preschoolers (ages 2–5). Fairy tales such as Cinderella, Snow White, and Hansel and Gretel are perfect for early readers (ages 5–8). Longer stories like Rikki-Tikki-Tavi and the Just So Stories suit independent readers (ages 8–12).

What are good bedtime stories for kids?

For bedtime, we recommend shorter stories with gentle endings: The Tale of Peter Rabbit, The Velveteen Rabbit, Thumbelina, and The Elves and the Shoemaker are all wonderful choices. Nursery rhymes and lullabies also make perfect bedtime reading — see our Lullabies collection for soothing songs and rhymes.

What kinds of stories are in this collection?

You'll find classic fairy tales (Cinderella, Rapunzel, Sleeping Beauty), Aesop's fables, Brothers Grimm tales, Hans Christian Andersen stories, Beatrix Potter tales, nursery rhymes from Mother Goose, folk tales from around the world (Japanese, Finnish, Hindu, Norwegian), American tall tales like Paul Bunyan, and holiday stories for Halloween, Thanksgiving, and Christmas.

Can I use these stories in the classroom or for homeschooling?

Absolutely. Our stories are widely used by teachers and homeschooling parents. All texts are in the public domain, so they can be read aloud, printed, or shared freely. Many of these stories appear in school reading curricula. For older students, see our Short Stories for Students page, which includes study guides and comprehension resources.
